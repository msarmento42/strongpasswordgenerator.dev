Passwords Passphrases FAQ

Answers to common questions about security, privacy, and how this site works

Do you store or see my passwords or passphrases?

No. All generation happens locally in your browser using the Web Crypto API. Nothing is uploaded or logged.

How are passwords generated?

We use window.crypto.getRandomValues to securely sample characters from your selected sets (uppercase, lowercase, numbers, symbols). You can choose length from 8–64.

Is this safe for important accounts?

Yes—when combined with best practices: long, unique secrets, a reputable password manager, and multi-factor authentication (2FA/MFA). Never reuse passwords.

Why use passphrases?

Random multi-word passphrases can deliver high entropy while being easier to remember. We recommend 6–8 words for most users.

What word list do you use? Do you filter offensive words?

Our GitHub Action builds wordlist.json from a large English vocabulary and removes offensive terms before publishing. The status line on the passphrase page shows how many words are loaded.

What is entropy?

Entropy (in bits) quantifies unpredictability. Each extra bit doubles the number of possibilities. Our passphrase page estimates entropy as words × log₂(wordListSize).

Do you track me?

We do not run first-party analytics. We show ads via Google AdSense, which may set cookies per Google’s policies. See our Privacy Policy.

Does it work offline?

Yes, core pages and assets are cached by a service worker after your first visit.

Can I contribute ideas or report an issue?

Absolutely—open an issue on the GitHub repo or contact us via the site footer links.
